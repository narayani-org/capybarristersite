CAPYBARRISTER PRIVACY POLICY

Last Updated: June 08 2025

1. INTRODUCTION

This Privacy Policy describes how Capybarrister ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, processes, and protects your personal information when you visit our website at www.capybarrister.com or use our Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) legal technology solutions (collectively, the "Services").

Capybarrister is committed to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of our users' information, particularly given the sensitive nature of legal data. This policy complies with applicable data protection laws, including the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011, and incorporates principles from international data protection frameworks.

2.1 Personal Information

We collect the following categories of personal information:

Registration and Account Information:
  • Full name and professional title
  • Email address and phone number
  • Law firm or organization name
  • Professional bar registration details
  • Billing and payment information
  • Login credentials (username and encrypted password)
Professional Information:
  • Practice areas and specializations
  • Years of experience
  • Professional certifications and qualifications
  • Bar association memberships
Service Usage Data:
  • Case management data and legal documents
  • Client information processed through our platform
  • Time tracking and billing records
  • Legal research queries and results
  • Calendar and scheduling information
  • Communication logs within the platform

2.2 Technical Information

We automatically collect technical information including:

  • IP address and geolocation data
  • Browser type, version, and language settings
  • Operating system and device information
  • Log files recording system activity
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies
  • Usage patterns and feature utilization metrics

2.3 Sensitive Legal Data

Our platform may process highly sensitive information including:

  • Attorney-client privileged communications
  • Confidential legal documents and case files
  • Court filings and legal precedents
  • Financial information related to legal matters
  • Personal data of your clients (when you use our services)

6. DATA SECURITY MEASURES

6.1 Technical Safeguards

  • End-to-end encryption for data transmission
  • Advanced encryption standards (AES-256) for data at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication for user accounts
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Secure cloud infrastructure with 99.9% uptime guarantee

6.2 Administrative Safeguards

  • Role-based access controls
  • Regular security training for employees
  • Incident response and breach notification procedures
  • Data loss prevention systems
  • Regular backup and disaster recovery protocols

6.3 Physical Safeguards

  • Secure data centers with biometric access controls
  • 24/7 physical security monitoring
  • Environmental controls and fire suppression systems
  • Redundant power and network connections

7. DATA RETENTION

7.1 Active Accounts

We retain personal information for active accounts as long as necessary to provide services and comply with legal obligations.

7.2 Inactive Accounts

For inactive accounts, we retain data for:

  • User account information: 3 years after account closure
  • Legal documents and case files: 7 years (or as required by applicable legal retention requirements)
  • Billing and payment records: 10 years for tax and audit purposes
  • System logs and technical data: 2 years

7.3 Legal Requirements

Certain information may be retained longer to comply with:

  • Bar association record-keeping requirements
  • Court-mandated retention periods
  • Tax and financial regulations
  • Ongoing legal proceedings
